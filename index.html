01

The Stack — Top to Bottom

Eight terms cover 80% of any AI conversation. They nest — each one is a subset of the one above it.

01

Artificial Intelligence

AI

Software that performs tasks that normally require human intelligence — language, recognition, planning, decision-making. The umbrella term.

02

Machine Learning

ML

A subset of AI where the system learns patterns from data instead of being explicitly programmed. Dominant approach for 15 years.

03

Deep Learning

Machine learning using neural networks with many layers. The breakthrough behind virtually all modern AI.

04

Neural Network

A mathematical model loosely inspired by the brain — layers of "neurons" with adjustable weights that learn patterns.

05

Generative AI

Models that create new content — text, images, audio, video, code — rather than just classifying or predicting from existing data.

06

Large Language Model

LLM

A generative model trained on huge text corpora to predict the next word, scaled until it can write, reason, and converse. The thing behind ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini.

07

Foundation Model

A large, general-purpose model trained once on broad data, then adapted to many downstream uses. "Foundation" because everything else builds on it.

08

Frontier Model

The current most-capable generation of foundation models — what the industry pushes the limits with (Claude Opus, GPT-5, Gemini Ultra).

02

How Models Work — The Machinery

If you understand these eight, you can hold your own with anyone selling you an AI capability — or asking you for a budget.

09

Training

The (very expensive) process of feeding a model data so it learns. A frontier model costs $100M–$1B+ and burns months of GPU time.

10

Inference

Running a trained model to actually answer a question. What happens every time you press send. Cheap per call, huge in aggregate.

11

Parameters

The internal "dials" the model adjusts during training. Frontier LLMs have hundreds of billions to trillions. More ≠ always better.

12

Tokens

How text is chopped up before the model sees it. Roughly ¾ of a word each. Vendor pricing is almost always per token.

13

Context Window

How much text a model can "see" at once. 200K tokens ≈ 500 pages. Bigger context = more documents in one shot.

14

Embeddings

Numerical fingerprints of meaning. The model turns every word, sentence, or document into a vector so it can compare ideas mathematically.

15

Transformer

The neural-network architecture (invented at Google in 2017) behind every modern LLM. The "T" in GPT.

16

Compute

Raw processing power — almost always GPUs from Nvidia. The scarce, expensive resource that gates the entire industry.

03

How You Use Them — Interaction

Most of the value isn't in the model itself — it's in how you wire it into your data, your tools, and your workflow.

17

Prompt

The instructions you give the model. "Prompt engineering" is the craft of writing them well — it matters more than people expect.

18

System Prompt

Hidden instructions a developer sets to shape behavior across every conversation — "you are a tax-research assistant…"

19

RAG

Retrieval-Augmented Generation

Let the model look things up in your documents before answering, instead of relying only on training data. The standard pattern for working with private data.

20

Fine-Tuning

Continuing to train an existing model on your data so it specializes. Cheaper than training from scratch; less needed now that prompts and RAG are powerful.

21

Agent

An AI that takes multi-step actions on its own — plans, calls tools, checks results, iterates. "Agentic" workflows are the 2025–26 frontier.

22

Tool Use

Function Calling

A model's ability to call external software — search, calculators, databases, APIs — rather than just produce text. What makes an agent useful.

23

MCP

Model Context Protocol

An open standard (from Anthropic, now industry-adopted) for connecting AI models to data sources and tools. Think "USB-C for AI integrations."

24

Multimodal

Models that handle multiple input types at once: text + images + audio + video. Increasingly the default for frontier models.

04

Business & Safety — Buyer Vocabulary

The vocabulary that shows up in vendor pitches, compliance reviews, and 'is it safe to deploy this?' conversations.

25

Hallucination

When a model produces plausible-sounding but false content. The #1 reliability problem in production AI.

26

Benchmark

A standardized test for model capability — MMLU, GPQA, SWE-Bench, HumanEval. Useful, but watch for "benchmaxing."

27

Open vs. Closed

Closed: you call a vendor's API (Claude, GPT). Open weights: you download and run the model yourself (Llama, Mistral, DeepSeek).

28

Alignment

The work of making models behave the way humans want them to — helpful, honest, harmless. Both a research field and a product property.

29

RLHF

Reinforcement Learning from Human Feedback

The standard technique that turns a raw LLM into a polite, useful assistant.

30

Guardrails

Rules and filters that constrain what a model will do — refuse certain content, redact PII, stay in-policy.

31

Jailbreak

A prompt that tricks a model into bypassing its guardrails. The cat-and-mouse game of AI security.

32

Inference Cost

What you pay per token to use a model. Frontier models run roughly $1–$75 per million tokens; pricing drops ~10× per year.

05

The Labs — Who Builds the Models

Eight labs build virtually every frontier model. Knowing who builds what unlocks every model name you'll hear.

33

Anthropic

US · Founded 2021

Builds the Claude family (Opus, Sonnet, Haiku). Safety- and reliability-focused. Backed by Amazon and Google.

34

OpenAI

US · Founded 2015

Builds GPT and ChatGPT, plus DALL-E (image) and Sora (video). Backed primarily by Microsoft.

35

Google DeepMind

US/UK · Google

Google's AI division. Builds Gemini, Veo (video), Imagen (image), and the underlying transformer research.

36

Meta AI

US · Meta Platforms

Builds the Llama family. The leader in "open-weights" models you can download and run yourself.

37

xAI

US · Founded 2023

Elon Musk's lab. Builds Grok, integrated into X (formerly Twitter). Fewer guardrails by design.

38

Mistral AI

France · Founded 2023

European frontier lab. Strong open-weights options; the EU's flagship AI player.

39

DeepSeek

China · Founded 2023

Chinese lab famous for matching frontier performance at a small fraction of the training cost. Open weights.

40

Alibaba (Qwen)

China · Alibaba Cloud

Strong multilingual frontier models from a Chinese tech giant. Open weights; popular outside the US.
